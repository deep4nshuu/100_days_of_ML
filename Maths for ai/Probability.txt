# Probability : It is a branch of mathematcis that deal with calculating the likelihood of a given event to occur
- The Probability value lies b.w 0(impossible) & 1(certain)
- Probability of an event to occur = No of ways an event occur / Total no of outcomes

# Random Variable : It is a numerical descriptn of outcomes of Random events OR a Random var maps outcomes of random events to numerical values
Ex: Consider Tossing a coin where Random var is x & for Random events of tossing(H/T) have Possible val as 0 & 1

# Types of Random Variable: Discrete(takes only Discrete or distinct val) & Continuous(takes any val in a giv range)

# Probability Distribution : For a random var, it describes how the probabilities are distributed over values of random var

# Normal Distribution : It is an arrangement of dataset in which most of the data pts lie in the middle of the range & the rest taper off symmetrically toward either extreme
It is also known as Gaussian Distribution(have symmetrical curve)
And value of Mean,Mode,Median is all at same centre pt of graph

# Skewness : A data is considered Skewed when the Distribution curve appears distorted or Skewed either to the left or right in a statistical distributn
1. Positively Skewed(more data on left) : Curve high to right where mean,Median,Mode(top of curve) follows from left to right
2. Negatively Skewed(more data on right) : Curve high to left where mean,Median,Mode(top of curve) follows from right to left

# Poisson Distribution : It is a Probability distributn that measures how many times an event is likely to occur within a specified period of time.
It is used to understand independent events that occur at a constant rate within a given interval of time.
- Formula : p(x) = (e^-(lambda))*(lambda)^x / x(fact.)  where x(no of times event occurs), lambda(mean no of events), e(euler no-2.71828)
